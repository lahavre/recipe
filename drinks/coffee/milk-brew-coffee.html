Cold Steep · Coffee in Milk

Milk Brew Coffee

Ground coffee steeped directly in milk overnight — no dilution needed, just a heavy-bodied, naturally sweet brew that's excellent over ice cream.

~16 hr steep No extra equipment Serve chilled
4 servings

    Method

    1. Combine coffee and milk Add the ground coffee to a bottle or server with at least 500ml capacity. Pour in the milk, measuring by weight rather than volume for accuracy.
    2. Steep overnight in the fridge Refrigerate for about 16 hours (overnight is easiest) so the coffee slowly infuses into the milk.
      Steep 16:00:00
    3. Strain Set a paper filter into a coffee dripper over a clean vessel. Pour the steeped milk through slowly and let it filter completely. Don't squeeze or press the grounds — squeezing pulls out unwanted, bitter flavours along with the good ones.
    4. Serve Serve chilled as is, or pour over vanilla ice cream for an affogato-style treat.

    Good to know

    • Light roast beans generally work better than medium or dark roast here — they hold up against the milk's richness without turning muddy.
    • Steeping the grounds directly in the milk (rather than in a bag or sachet) gives the strongest flavour and the fastest extraction.
    • Never squeeze a filter bag or paper during straining — squeezing extracts bitter, "negative" flavours along with the good ones.
    • Expect a heavy-bodied, naturally sweet drink thanks to the overnight infusion with milk's natural sugars.
