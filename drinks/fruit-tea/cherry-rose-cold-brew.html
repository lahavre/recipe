Cherry Rose Cold Brew

A ruby-pink, tart-sweet infusion of hibiscus and rose with blackcurrant and peach — slow-steeped cold in a 1L pitcher and strained over ice.

1L pitcher or jar 6–12 h steep Caffeine-free Best served cold
4 glasses

    Method

    1. Add the tea Add the cherry rose tea straight into your pitcher or jar. Break up any large peach or fruit chunks so they soak evenly.
    2. Add the fresh fruit Slice the peach thin (fresh or canned — both work) and drop it in. Add a few sliced strawberries too if you'd like a fuller berry note. Canned peach is already sweet, so taste before sweetening later.
    3. Fill with cold water Pour in 1L of cold filtered water. The tea, flowers, and fruit will float and steep freely in the water.
    4. Swirl, cap, and chill Give it a gentle swirl so everything is wetted, cover with a lid, and refrigerate. This blend is hibiscus- and blackcurrant-heavy, so it runs tart: 6 hours gives a brighter, lighter cup; overnight gives a fuller, deeper, more sour one. Swirl once midway to keep the fruit submerged.
      Steep 8:00:00
    5. Add the lemon near the end (optional) If using lemon, add the thin slices in the last 2–3 hours of steeping so the peel doesn't turn bitter over the full brew. It brightens the hibiscus and keeps the cup refreshing.
      Lemon in 2:30:00
    6. Sweeten to taste (optional) Taste first — but this blend is properly tart, so it usually wants a little sweetening. Dissolve rock sugar or honey in a splash of warm water to make a syrup, cool it, then stir it in (cold liquid won't dissolve either one well). Or sweeten by the glass so the batch keeps longer.
    7. Pour and serve Strain through a fine sieve into a glass of ice. The sieve holds back the fruit and petals for a clean, ruby-pink cup.

    Good to know

    • Straining only happens when you pour — all the steeping happens loose in the pitcher.
    • This one is the tart one. Hibiscus (洛神花) plus blackcurrant means it really wants sweetening — more so than a floral blend. Plan on it, and lean to the 6-hour steep if you're sour-sensitive.
    • The coconut jelly (椰果) won't really brew — it just rehydrates and settles at the bottom. Harmless; scoop a few pieces into your glass to chew if you like.
    • Canned peach already sits in sweet syrup — taste before adding sugar, or rinse the slices if you're watching sweetness. A spoonful of the can syrup also works in place of a separate sugar syrup.
    • With fresh fruit in it, drink within 1–2 days and give it a sniff before drinking on day 2.
