Crispy Pork Belly Siu Yuk

Cantonese-style roast pork belly with bubbled, shattering crackling — a dry overnight rest and a two-stage roast are what get the skin there.

Oven roasted 10–12 hr dry-brine ~1 hr active time Serves 4

    Method

    1. Parboil the pork Bring a pot of water to a boil — enough to submerge the pork belly. Place it skin-side down and cook for 1 minute, then flip and cook the meat side for another 30–45 seconds, just until the colour changes.
      Skin side 1:00
    2. Shock and clean the skin Transfer the pork straight into a bowl of ice water. Scrape the skin firmly with a knife held upright, working sideways to remove any surface debris, then pat it completely dry. Poke holes all over the skin, through its full thickness, with a fork or toothpicks — the more holes you poke now, the more bubbly and crackly the skin will become later.
    3. Score and season the meat Flip the belly skin-side down. Cut two long slashes into the meat without piercing the skin, then score perpendicular cuts to create a diamond pattern. Mix the dry rub and work it into the scored meat and crevices, keeping it off the skin entirely. Stir the remaining rub into the bean sauce and sugar, and rub that marinade over the meat as well.
    4. Wrap and dry-brine the skin Wrap the sides of the belly in aluminium foil, skin facing up and left exposed. Sprinkle the salt over the skin and let it sit for 5 minutes, then wipe it off — this draws out surface moisture for a crispier finish. Brush the skin with the rice vinegar.
      Salt draw 5:00
    5. Rest overnight Refrigerate the pork uncovered for at least 10–12 hours (overnight is easiest). This dry-out time is essential for the skin to crisp properly in the oven — don't rush or skip it.
      Dry-brine 12:00:00
    6. Roast at low heat Preheat your oven to 190°C (205°C for a toaster oven). Set a roasting rack over a pan with the water, and place the pork on the rack, skin-side up. Roast for 20–25 minutes.
      Low roast 20:00
    7. Finish at high heat Raise the oven to 220°C (230°C for a toaster oven) and continue roasting for another 30–35 minutes, watching the skin closely in the final 10–15 minutes as it blisters and crackles.
      High roast 30:00
    8. Rest before carving Let the pork rest for a full hour before cutting into it. This lets the juices redistribute and makes for cleaner slices and crisper crackling.
      Rest 1:00:00

    Good to know

    • Poke as many holes as you can stand to — more holes means more bubbled, shattering crackling once it's roasted.
    • The overnight dry-out in the fridge is non-negotiable for good crackling. Don't shorten it much below 10 hours if you can help it.
    • Salting then wiping the skin, followed by a coat of white rice vinegar, both pull moisture out of the skin before it ever sees the oven — moisture is the enemy of crackling.
    • Keep the dry rub and marinade off the skin entirely. Any spice or moisture there will stop it from crisping.
    • Resist cutting into it right away — the full hour of resting keeps the juices in and makes the crackling easier to shatter cleanly once it's cooled slightly.
