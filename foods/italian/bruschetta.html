Bruschetta

      Notes

      A high-quality loaf with a compact interior — pane di casa or a dense sourdough — works best. Too many big holes and the bread turns soggy under the tomato juice.

      Go easy on the garlic rub, since the tomato mixture already has garlic in it. You want a light kiss of flavour on the toast, not an overpowering one.

      Don't let the tomato mixture sit longer than an hour or so — it turns watery and loses its fresh bite. Five minutes to an hour is the sweet spot.

      Serve straight away on a flat plate — bruschetta doesn't wait well once it's assembled. For a twist, try fresh mozzarella, burrata, or a balsamic drizzle on top.
