Eggplant Tomato Pasta

No canned tomatoes here — eggplant fried low and slow in garlic-basil oil, tossed with a quick fresh tomato sauce and finished with cacioricotta.

      Notes

      Fresh tomato, marinated briefly with salt and sugar, gives a brighter and more concentrated sauce than canned tomato ever can for a quick-cook dish like this one.

      Fry the eggplant skin-side down low and slow, without peeling it. Rushing this step is the main way this dish goes wrong — the skin needs time to soften and the flesh needs time to soak up the garlic-basil oil.

      Cacioricotta is a firm, salty Italian sheep's-milk cheese. If you can't find it, pecorino romano or parmesan are both good substitutes.

      Straining the sauce removes the tomato skins for a silky, restaurant-style finish — skip this step for a more rustic, chunkier sauce if you prefer.
