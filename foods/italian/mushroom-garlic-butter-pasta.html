Mushroom Garlic Butter Pasta

A one-pan mushroom pasta built like a professional's formula: mushrooms seared dry until deeply browned, then bathed in a garlic butter base rounded out with a splash of umami-rich dashi.

    Method

      Notes

      Tear the mushrooms into pieces by hand rather than slicing them — the ragged edges brown more and give better texture than a clean knife-cut surface.

      Let the mushrooms sit undisturbed once they hit the pan. Resist stirring too soon; a proper sear is what gives this dish its depth, and stirring too early just steams them.

      No white dashi (shirodashi) on hand? Swap in a light chicken or dashi stock with a small pinch of salt, or dissolve a little dashi powder or a fraction of a stock cube into the water.

      Keep a splash of the pasta water aside — if the sauce looks tight before the pasta finishes cooking, loosen it with a spoonful.
