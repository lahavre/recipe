Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

The classic Roman pantry pasta — garlic and chili sizzled gold in olive oil, whisked with starchy pasta water into a silky, clinging sauce. No cream, no cheese, just technique.

    Method

      Notes

      Crush the garlic rather than chopping it — crushing ruptures more of the cell walls and releases a punchier flavour into the oil.

      The starchy pasta water is what turns oil and water into a silky, clinging sauce — don't skip reserving a cup of it before draining.

      Traditional aglio e olio skips cheese entirely, but a dusting of pecorino or a scattering of toasted breadcrumbs (for a dairy-free crunch) are both welcome finishing touches.

      A couple of anchovy fillets melted into the oil alongside the garlic add a savoury depth if you'd like to riff on the classic. A little lemon zest at the end brightens it up nicely too.
