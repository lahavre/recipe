Spaghetti Puttanesca

A bold, salty-savory Italian classic — olives, capers, and anchovies simmered into a glossy tomato sauce with garlic and chili.

      Notes

      Crushing the olives with the flat of your knife, rather than leaving them whole, helps release more of their flavour into the oil as they cook.

      Include the chili seeds if you want real heat — leave them out for a milder sauce.

      Anchovies dissolve fastest and taste best when stirred in off the highest heat. Letting them sit too long over a hard simmer brings out an unwanted fishy edge.

      This sauce is naturally salty from the olives, capers, and anchovies — taste before reaching for extra salt.
